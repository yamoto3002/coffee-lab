# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Coffee Lab is for home roasters, small-batch coffee practitioners, and curious beginners who record experiments while working at a roaster or reviewing results later. The primary user is often standing, one-handed, and time-sensitive during a roast; the secondary use is deliberate comparison and analysis on a larger screen.

## Product Purpose

Coffee Lab connects green-bean inventory, roast events, tasting records, and analysis into one dependable experimental journal. Success means a first-time user can identify the next useful action immediately, an experienced user can capture First Crack, Second Crack, and Drop without hesitation, and every saved record remains understandable and recoverable across local, offline, and Google Sheets states.

## Positioning

Coffee Lab turns each roast into an evidence-backed next experiment without making users learn a laboratory information system.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, and warm. The product should feel technically trustworthy and quietly experimental, like a well-kept instrument bench in a dim roastery—not sterile, theatrical, or futuristic for its own sake. Japanese is the default product language; established coffee terms may remain in English when they are the clearer industry convention, with concise Japanese context where needed.

## Anti-references

- Marketing landing pages with hero slogans, decorative metrics, or conversion-first hierarchy.
- Generic AI/SaaS dashboards made from repeated icon cards.
- Neon cyberpunk interfaces, purple gradients, decorative grids, glassmorphism, and constant glow.
- Apple or Google surface mimicry without Coffee Lab's own operational logic.
- Dense specialist tooling that assumes every user already understands roast science terminology.

## Design Principles

1. **The next useful action is visible.** Every screen makes the current task and its safest next step obvious.
2. **Record first, explain in context.** Live Roast protects speed; supporting definitions appear beside the decisions that need them.
3. **State is a sentence, not a color.** Saving, syncing, pending, offline, success, and failure always use explicit language and recovery guidance.
4. **One experiment, one source of truth.** Date-only values remain `YYYY-MM-DD`; roast milestones remain elapsed `MM:SS`; duplicated UI never creates duplicated data ownership.
5. **Mobile is the working surface.** The phone flow is designed for one-handed, interruption-prone use, while desktop earns its extra space through comparison and analysis.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. All primary workflows must be keyboard-operable, focus-visible, understandable without color, and usable with reduced motion. Touch targets are at least 44 by 44 CSS pixels. Japanese body text prioritizes legibility over density, and specialist abbreviations receive nearby explanations or expanded labels.
