## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** The product currently reads as an AI-generated “coffee lab” concept before it reads as a dependable roast tool. The combination of cyan/magenta accents, gradient text, backdrop blur, decorative grid, repeated glass cards, tracked uppercase labels, and glow overwhelms otherwise solid domain modeling.

**Deterministic scan:** Seven actionable findings: a side-stripe accent in `src/app/beans/page.tsx`, overused Inter declaration and gradient text plus bounce easing in `src/app/globals.css`, and three gray-on-colored-background combinations in roast and tasting screens. No finding was dismissed as a false positive at baseline.

**Browser evidence:** Desktop and mobile production-preview screenshots were inspected at 1440×900 and 390×844. Browser mutation/overlay injection was not used; the CLI detector and direct screenshots were the fallback evidence.

## Overall Impression

The app has strong domain bones: local-first messaging, separate roast milestones, useful empty-state guidance, and a complete route set. Its biggest opportunity is to replace “lab aesthetic” decoration with an operational hierarchy that makes the next roast action, current timer state, and save/sync state easier to scan.

## What's Working

- Local preservation and pending-sync behavior are stated in user-facing language.
- First Crack, Second Crack, Drop, development time, and date-only semantics remain separate.
- Desktop navigation is labeled and mobile navigation keeps the major destinations visible.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Decoration competes with the workflow

The home, settings, and Live Roast screens use glass cards, gradients, glow, decorative grid lines, wide shadows, and multiple accent hues. This reduces scan speed and makes state colors feel ornamental. Replace those effects with flat tonal layers, structural dividers, and one warm action color.

### [P1] Home does not establish a compact operational priority

Brand copy and the large AI Coach container occupy more space than the current task. The first viewport should answer: what is due, what was recently recorded, and what can I do now?

### [P1] Mobile Live Roast is visually top-heavy

The save action sits at the top while the timer, milestones, and heat/air controls extend below the fold behind persistent navigation. Preserve the timer as the visual anchor and keep the current primary action reachable without obscuring controls.

### [P1] Mixed terminology and unexplained abbreviations

“Science. Flavor. Evolution.”, “READY FOR CHARGE”, “SETUP”, “DEV”, “NEW EXPERIMENT”, and Japanese labels coexist without a clear language policy. Keep established roast terms where useful, but make instructions and state Japanese-first and explain DEV beside the value.

### [P1] The component vocabulary changes across routes

Similar buttons, status pills, cards, inputs, and active-navigation states use different radii, colors, shadows, and focus treatments. A small canonical set is needed before route-by-route polishing.

## Persona Red Flags

- **Jordan (first-timer):** The first screen foregrounds branding and coach terminology before showing a simple starting path. DEV and cloud configuration language require prior knowledge.
- **Sam (keyboard/low vision):** Several compact icon controls and small labels rely on subtle color. Focus treatment and input labeling are not uniform across route-local components.
- **Casey (distracted mobile user):** Live Roast's key controls compete vertically, while a fixed navigation layer consumes the lower thumb zone.

## Minor Observations

- The empty states are helpful but visually over-produced.
- Current mobile navigation repeats “焙煎” for both destination and center action.
- Settings renders unrelated concerns as equally prominent stacked containers.

## Questions to Consider

- Can the app feel experimental through its data and interaction model rather than through glow and gradients?
- Which three pieces of information must remain visible while a roast is actively running?
- Can every sync message be reduced to state, consequence, and recovery action?
